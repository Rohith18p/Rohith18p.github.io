Obstacle-Aware Manipulation with PincherX100

Stage 1 - MATLAB Simulation: Generated trajectory with waypoints for smooth paths to transition between start to goal. Calculated the joint positions corresponding to each waypoint using Multiple Inverse Kinematics approaches - Analytical solution, Builtin solver, Gradient Descent. Implemented Obstacle Avoidance using Geometric approach to generate collision-free paths. Defined a safety-offset circle around obstacles and generated a trajectory with a straight-line, a curved path along the circle, and a final straight line to the goal. In addition generated collision free paths using A* method by modeling the workspace as a grid, marking obstacles as restricted zones, and computed an optimal collision-free path.

Stage 2 - Real-World Implementation: A vision setup comprising an overhead camera and AprilTags to dynamically detect object, goal, and obstacle positions is used. Trajectories obtained by implementing afore mentioned methods were fed to the PincherX100 manipulator for precise pick-and-place actions.
