Holonomic Mobile Robot Bases

I developed three distinct mobile bases to cater to different robotic applications, each tested extensively to ensure optimal performance.

1. Four-Wheeled Mecanum Drive: Designed for omnidirectional movement, this base utilizes mecanum wheels to achieve smooth navigation in any direction, including lateral and diagonal motion.

2. Four-Wheeled Omni Wheel Drive: Built for enhanced maneuverability, this base employs omni-wheels, offering 360-degree movement while maintaining stability and control.

3. Three-Wheeled Omni Wheel Drive: A compact and efficient design, the tri-wheel base enables full 360-degree control with a unique motor configuration that differs from conventional 4-wheel systems, ensuring precise movement even in constrained spaces.

All bases are powered by encoder motors, allowing for accurate feedback and control. I implemented PID control algorithms for precise motion management, utilizing a PS joystick or RC controller for user-friendly operation. The combination of encoder feedback and PID tuning resulted in highly responsive and smooth navigation across all platforms.

Particularly noteworthy is the tri-wheel omni drive, where achieving precise 360-degree control presented unique challenges due to its distinct motor configuration. By optimizing motor control algorithms, I successfully ensured seamless omnidirectional movement with the same level of accuracy as its 4-wheel counterparts.

These mobile bases demonstrate versatility and reliability, making them suitable for a variety of robotic applications, from autonomous navigation to industrial automation. The project showcases my expertise in mechatronics, control systems, and motion dynamics.
