About Me

I’m a robotics researcher focused on developing autonomy stack that enable robots to act intelligently and adaptively in the real world. My path into robotics began with building — mobile robots, manipulators, bionic hands, and competition-ready platforms. These early experiences laid the foundation for deeper research into robot learning, real-time planning, SLAM, and perception. Over the years, I’ve engineered systems that blend hardware and algorithms — from autonomous harvesters in vertical farms to multi-robot SLAM deployments using Spot, AgileX, and Unitree robots.


Currently, at the Autonomy and Intelligence Lab at Northeastern University, I work on risk-aware trajectory planning, and safety guaranteed control strategies with contingency planning. My work emphasizes real-world deployment and robustness under uncertainty, combining ROS2 and hardware-in-the-loop systems. My long-term vision is to build robust, generalizable autonomy stacks that operate safely, intelligently, and adaptively — from structured labs to chaotic real-world environments.
